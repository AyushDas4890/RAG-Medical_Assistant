title: Hypertension in adults — diagnosis and management

Diagnose hypertension if the clinic blood pressure is 140/90 mmHg or higher and ambulatory or home blood pressure monitoring confirms an average of 135/85 mmHg or higher. Stage 1 hypertension is a clinic reading of 140/90 to 159/99 mmHg. Stage 2 hypertension is a clinic reading of 160/100 mmHg or higher.

Offer antihypertensive drug treatment to adults under 80 with stage 1 hypertension who have one or more of: target organ damage, established cardiovascular disease, renal disease, diabetes, or an estimated 10-year cardiovascular risk of 10% or more. Offer drug treatment to people of any age with stage 2 hypertension.

For adults with type 2 diabetes of any age or origin, or for adults under 55 who are not of Black African or African-Caribbean origin, offer a first-line angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor or an angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB). For adults aged 55 or over without diabetes, or for people of Black African or African-Caribbean origin without diabetes, offer a calcium-channel blocker (CCB) first.

Do not combine an ACE inhibitor with an ARB. If blood pressure is not controlled on step 1, add a CCB or a thiazide-like diuretic. The treatment target for adults under 80 is a clinic blood pressure below 140/90 mmHg; for adults aged 80 and over the target is below 150/90 mmHg. Check renal function and serum electrolytes before and after starting an ACE inhibitor or ARB.
